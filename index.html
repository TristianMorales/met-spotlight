Featured Artists

Meet the artists whose works are currently on display at The Met, each contributing unique perspectives to our understanding of human creativity.

"I want to make paintings so beautiful that people want to lick them."

Kerry James Marshall

b. 1955, Birmingham, Alabama

Known for his large-scale paintings depicting Black figures in everyday settings, Marshall's work addresses the absence of Black subjects in Western art history while celebrating African American life and culture.

Read Full Profile

"I want to create infinity rooms and polka dot everything."

Yayoi Kusama

b. 1929, Matsumoto, Japan

A pioneering artist whose immersive installations and sculptures explore themes of repetition, pattern, and infinity. Her iconic polka dots and infinity rooms have captivated audiences worldwide.

Read Full Profile

"Art is about raising questions, not providing answers."

Ai Weiwei

b. 1957, Beijing, China

Contemporary artist and activist whose work spans sculpture, installation, and photography. His pieces often comment on Chinese politics and culture while addressing universal themes of freedom and human rights.

Read Full Profile

"I use art to make the past speak to the present."

Kara Walker

b. 1969, Stockton, California

Known for her provocative silhouette installations, Walker examines race, gender, and violence in American history. Her work confronts uncomfortable truths about the legacy of slavery and its ongoing impact.

Read Full Profile

"Art is something that makes you breathe with a different kind of happiness."

Takashi Murakami

b. 1962, Tokyo, Japan

Contemporary artist who blurs the boundaries between high and low art, incorporating elements of anime, manga, and consumer culture into his colorful, pop-inspired sculptures and paintings.

Read Full Profile

"The moment you cheat for the sake of beauty, you know you're an artist."

David Hockney

b. 1937, Bradford, England

One of the most influential painters of the 20th century, known for his pool paintings, photocollages, and stage designs. His work celebrates the beauty of everyday life with vibrant colors and innovative techniques.

Read Full Profile
